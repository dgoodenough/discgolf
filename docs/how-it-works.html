How the DGPT points system works

← back to the forecast

The Disc Golf Pro Tour crowns its season champion at the Powerball Cup (the DGPT Championship). Who gets to play in it is decided by the Powerball DGPT World Standings — a season-long points race. This page explains how the points work, what counts, and how a player gets in.

1 · Every event awards points by finishing place

Each points event awards points on a fixed curve: the winner gets the most, and every place after that gets a bit less, with points awarded deep into the field (MPO past 140th place, FPO to about 50th). Bigger events award more — the same curve is scaled up by event class:

Event classWin awardsScale
DGPT (Elite Series)150base curve
DGPT+200base × 4⁄3
DGPT Playoffs250base × 5⁄3
PDGA Major300base × 2
Doubles Championship137.5each partner gets half the combined points of the two singles places their team finish spans
JomezPro Series20flat bonus: 20 for a win, 10 for top 5, 5 for top 10

Ties split the pot: everyone in a tie gets the average of the points for the places the tie covers (two players tied 2nd each get the average of 2nd- and 3rd-place points).

2 · Not every finish counts

A player's season total isn't everything they earned — each class of event has its own cap, so skipping events (or having a bad one) eventually stops hurting:

ClassWhat counts
DGPT + DGPT+ (incl. doubles)best 10 finishes (of ~15 events)
Playoffs (GMC + MVP Open)both count, always
Majorsbest 2 — MPO has 3 eligible majors, FPO has 4
JomezPro bonusall of it, on top

That's 14 counted finishes plus bonus, for a theoretical maximum of 2,800 points. Wrinkles: the Heinola Open awards no FPO points (it ends four days before the U.S. Women's Championship), and the USDGC awards no points because it falls after the points season ends at the MVP Open. In the forecast's expanded player view, finishes that don't count are struck through.

3 · How you make the Powerball Cup

The Cup field is 32 MPO / 20 FPO. Three ways in:

  • Automatic bid — finish the season top 28 MPO / top 18 FPO in the standings.
  • Playoff run — the top 4 MPO / 2 FPO finishers at the MVP Open who missed the standings cut take the last spots.
  • Winner's invite — win any DGPT Elite or Major singles event and you're in regardless, added as a bottom seed without bumping anyone (doubles and Jomez wins don't qualify).

The playoffs themselves are gated by the standings: the Green Mountain Championship field is the top 100 MPO / 50 FPO after Worlds (expanding to 120/60 if it doesn't fill), and the MVP Open field is the top 72/36 after GMC plus the top 8/4 GMC finishers from outside that line. So a hot September can carry a player from 90th place all the way into the Cup.

4 · What the forecast does with all this

The forecast simulates the rest of the season 100,000 times: each player's scores are drawn from their PDGA rating, fields come from real registration lists (or participation patterns where registration isn't open), live events are projected from the holes actually remaining, and every rule above is applied to every simulated season. The percentages you see are just how often each outcome happened. Full methodology and source: github.com/dgoodenough/discgolf.
